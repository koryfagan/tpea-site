Where Safety Systems Fail

Most SME safety frameworks satisfy documentation requirements. They do not necessarily withstand enforcement scrutiny.

What Exists

  • Policies and procedural manuals
  • Hazard and incident registers
  • Training records
  • Contractor induction processes

What Breaks Under Scrutiny

  • Escalation thresholds are undefined
  • Officer oversight is informal
  • Board reporting lacks structure
  • Contractor controls are inconsistently evidenced
  • Documentation trails are fragmented

Compliance theatre creates internal comfort. Enforcement tests structure.

What the Stress Test Examines

The assessment reads your system as an external authority would — backward from an incident, not forward from intention.

Governance Accountability Mapping

Whether officer duties under the Health and Safety at Work Act are structurally embedded or assumed.

Escalation Architecture

Defined thresholds for notification, investigation, and board awareness. Informal judgement is treated as exposure.

Contractor Control Framework

Clarity of responsibility across overlapping duties. Evidence of supervision, verification, and integration.

Evidence Chain Integrity

Whether documentation demonstrates sequence, oversight, and corrective response. Gaps are read as absence.

Board Visibility & Reporting Logic

Frequency, structure, and depth of safety reporting to governance. Minutes and records are assessed as artefacts.

Incident Defensibility

How a serious harm event would be interpreted using the records currently in place.

Applied Context

Designed for New Zealand SMEs operating under director-level accountability.

  • 20–250 FTE
  • Multi-site or distributed operations
  • Machinery, plant, field, or contractor exposure
  • Officer liability under HSWA

Operational complexity increases faster than governance architecture. Informal systems that function during stability often fail under stress.

What You Receive

A formal Stress Test Report issued at director level.

  • Governance exposure mapping
  • Structural scoring bands
  • Observed documentation gaps
  • Escalation pathway assessment
  • Incident defensibility interpretation

The report does not prescribe remediation. It reads the system as it stands.
