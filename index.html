The Six Stress Tests

Each Stress Test applies institutional review to a defined operating domain.

They are finite, structured interventions.

They identify exposure.

They clarify capital impact.

They reset boundaries.

Safety Intelligence

Operational accountability and regulatory exposure mapping.

Identifies unmanaged compliance risk and structural safety blind spots.

Employment Risk Intelligence

Workforce liability, key-person dependency and contractual exposure analysis.

Tests resilience beyond informal arrangements.

Property and Asset Risk

Asset exposure, lease risk and capital structure review.

Examines long-term obligations against cash durability.

Supplier Dependency Audit

Concentration, substitution risk and operational reliance testing.

Identifies single points of failure across critical inputs.

Financial Architecture

Cash conversion discipline, forecast credibility and capital allocation structure.

Tests whether reported performance reflects economic reality.

Strategic Judgement Framework

Decision-rights clarity, capital gating and governance alignment.

Clarifies who decides, how capital is deployed and where risk accumulates.
