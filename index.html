About

The Private Equity Advantage is a decision discipline.

It applies institutional investment logic to operating businesses.

Most companies do not fail from lack of ambition. They fail from untested assumptions embedded in daily workflow — pricing tolerated without scrutiny, risk accepted without modelling, dependency unmeasured, governance informal. Most businesses are not fragile because they lack effort. They are fragile because they lack friction.

Private equity firms approach businesses differently. They assume error. They test cash conversion. They examine contractual leverage. They quantify operational fragility. They underwrite downside before underwriting growth.

Operating managers optimise within the system they know. Investors interrogate the system itself.

TPEA translates that posture into structured operating stress tests.

Each Stress Test isolates a core dimension of enterprise durability — capital efficiency, revenue quality, contractual exposure, dependency risk, operational concentration, and governance discipline. The objective is not advice. It is visibility.

Visibility changes behaviour.

When constraints are made explicit, allocation improves.
When fragility is surfaced, tolerance narrows.
When decision surfaces are clarified, discipline compounds.

Institutional scrutiny eventually arrives — through capital, competitors, regulators, or crisis. TPEA applies it deliberately, in advance.

TPEA was founded by Kory Fagan, a private equity investor and operator with more than 25 years’ experience in the New Zealand mid-market, including as a founding partner of NZ Equity Partners. Over that period, he observed that enterprise risk is typically absorbed quietly long before it is recognised formally — and that valuation outcomes are determined by the discipline applied years earlier.

His work centres on capital allocation, governance architecture, and downside underwriting — the mechanics that determine durability before growth narratives are written.

TPEA does not provide strategy decks.
It does not offer transformation programs.
It does not optimise for expansion narratives.

It imposes structured review.

The output is a clearer understanding of how a business would perform under institutional scrutiny — before capital, before crisis, before negotiation.

Constraint precedes scale.
Durability precedes valuation.
Discipline precedes advantage.

When External Judgement Applies

External authorities do not assess intention.
They assess the record that existed before the event.

  • Insurer review following a material claim
  • Regulatory investigation after an incident
  • Employment dispute escalated beyond mediation
  • Bank credit reassessment under stress
  • Buyer diligence during transaction

Once escalation occurs, interpretation determines consequence.

Where SMEs Become Exposed

Structural weakness is rarely visible while everything appears to be working.

  • Policies exist but artefacts are inconsistent
  • Decisions are recorded but not structured
  • Risk ownership is assumed but not evidenced
  • Governance becomes informal under pressure

Exposure rarely originates in misconduct.
It originates in overlooked structure.
