Operating Principle
Discipline, then upside

Institutional capital rewards discipline, not optimism. Most businesses do not fail because of one dramatic error. They weaken incrementally.

Capital is misallocated. Governance drifts. Forecasts stretch. Risk concentrates. Cash tightens. Structural weakness compounds quietly.

The Six Stress Tests
Defined interventions

Each Stress Test applies institutional review to a defined operating domain. Finite scope. Structured output. Exposure identified. Capital impact clarified. Boundaries reset.

Safety Intelligence

Operational accountability and regulatory exposure mapping. Identifies unmanaged compliance risk and structural safety blind spots.

Employment Risk Intelligence

Workforce liability, key-person dependency and contractual exposure analysis. Tests resilience beyond informal arrangements.

Property and Asset Risk

Asset exposure, lease risk and capital structure review. Examines long-term obligations against cash durability.

Supplier Dependency Audit

Concentration, substitution risk and operational reliance testing. Identifies single points of failure across critical inputs.

Financial Architecture

Cash conversion discipline, forecast credibility and capital allocation structure. Tests whether reported performance reflects economic reality.

Strategic Judgement Framework

Decision-rights clarity, capital gating and governance alignment. Clarifies how capital is deployed and where exposure accumulates.
